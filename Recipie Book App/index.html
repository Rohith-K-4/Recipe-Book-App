Pasta Carbonara

    Ingredients

  • 200g pasta
  • 100g pancetta
  • 2 large eggs
  • 50g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Black pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook the pasta according to package instructions.
  2. Fry pancetta in a pan until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, Parmesan, and black pepper.
  4. Drain the pasta and quickly toss with the pancetta.
  5. Remove from heat and add the egg mixture, stirring quickly to coat the pasta.

Chocolate_chip_cookies

  • 1 cup butter, softened
  • 1 cup white sugar
  • 1 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • 2 teaspoons hot water
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 2 cups semisweet chocolate chips

Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C).
  2. Cream together the butter, white sugar, and brown sugar until smooth.
  3. Beat in the eggs one at a time, then stir in the vanilla.
  4. Dissolve baking soda in hot water, then add to batter along with salt.
  5. Stir in flour and chocolate chips.
  6. Drop by large spoonfuls onto ungreased pans.
  7. Bake for about 10 minutes in the preheated oven or until edges are nicely browned.

Vetetable stir fry

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (e.g., broccoli, bell peppers, carrots)
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon oyster sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan or wok over high heat.
  2. Add minced garlic and stir-fry for 30 seconds.
  3. Add mixed vegetables and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes until they are tender-crisp.
  4. Stir in soy sauce and oyster sauce, and cook for an additional 1-2 minutes.
  5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  6. Serve hot.

Idli

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup idli rice
  • 1/2 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice and urad dal separately and soak them for 4-5 hours.
  2. Grind them together with fenugreek seeds into a smooth batter, adding water as needed.
  3. Add salt and mix well. Allow it to ferment for 8-10 hours or overnight.
  4. Grease idli molds, pour the batter, and steam for about 10-12 minutes.
  5. Let them cool a bit, then remove from molds and serve with chutney and sambar.

Vada

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1-inch piece of ginger
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Soak urad dal for 4-5 hours. Drain the water.
  2. Grind dal with green chilies, ginger, and asafoetida into a thick batter.
  3. Add salt and mix well. Heat oil for frying.
  4. Wet your hands, take a portion of the batter, and shape it into a donut-like vada.
  5. Fry in hot oil until golden brown and crispy.
  6. Drain excess oil and serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Dosa

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup dosa rice
  • 1/4 cup urad dal (black gram)
  • 1/4 cup poha (flattened rice)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash rice, urad dal, and poha separately and soak them for 4-5 hours.
  2. Grind them into a smooth batter and add salt. Allow it to ferment for 8-10 hours.
  3. Heat a non-stick pan, pour a ladleful of batter, and spread it into a thin dosa.
  4. Drizzle oil, cook until crisp, and flip to cook the other side.
  5. Serve hot with chutney or sambar.

Coconut chutney

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup grated coconut
  • 2-3 green chilies
  • 1/2 cup roasted chana dal (split gram)
  • 1/2-inch piece of ginger
  • A few curry leaves
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed

Instructions

  1. In a blender, combine grated coconut, green chilies, roasted chana dal, ginger, and curry leaves.
  2. Add a little water and blend to a smooth paste.
  3. Season with salt and mix well.
  4. Serve with dosa or idli.

Venn pongal

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • 1/4 cup split yellow moong dal (lentils)
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter)
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon black peppercorns
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Wash rice and moong dal and cook them together with enough water until soft and mushy.
  2. In a pan, heat ghee and add cumin seeds, black peppercorns, grated ginger, and asafoetida.
  3. Saute for a minute and then add this tempering to the cooked rice and dal mixture.
  4. Add salt and mix well.
  5. Serve hot with sambar or coconut chutney.

Rice

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup long-grain rice
  • 2 cups water
  • A pinch of salt

Instructions

  1. Rinse rice thoroughly and drain.
  2. In a saucepan, combine rice, water, and a pinch of salt.
  3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 18-20 minutes or until rice is tender and water is absorbed.
  4. Remove from heat and let it sit for 5 minutes. Fluff with a fork and serve.

Sambar

Ingredients:

  • 1/2 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (e.g., carrots, potatoes, beans)
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon sambar powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • A few curry leaves
  • Tamarind pulp (size of a small lemon)
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook toor dal until soft and mash it.
  2. In a large pot, heat oil and add mustard seeds, fenugreek seeds, and asafoetida.
  3. Add onions, green chilies, and curry leaves. Saute until onions are translucent.
  4. Add tomatoes, sambar powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes.
  6. Add tamarind pulp and cooked dal. Bring to a boil and simmer until vegetables are tender.
  7. Serve hot with rice or idli.

Potato fry

Ingredients:

  • 4 potatoes, peeled and sliced
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds and cumin seeds.
  2. Add sliced potatoes and saute for a few minutes.
  3. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt.
  4. Cover and cook until potatoes are tender and crispy.
  5. Serve as a side dish.

Brinjal curry

Ingredients:

  • 4-5 brinjals, sliced
  • 2 onions, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon coriander powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add cumin seeds.
  2. Add onions and saute until translucent.
  3. Add ginger-garlic paste and saute for a minute.
  4. Add tomatoes, turmeric powder, red chili powder, coriander powder, and salt. Cook until tomatoes are soft.
  5. Add sliced brinjals and cook until they are tender and well-cooked.
  6. Serve as a side dish with rice or roti.

Paniyaram

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup idli batter
  • 1/2 cup finely chopped onions
  • 1/4 cup finely chopped green chilies
  • A few curry leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  • Oil for greasing the paniyaram pan
  • Salt to taste

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pan and add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, curry leaves, and chopped onions. Saute until onions are translucent.
  2. Add green chilies and saute for a minute.
  3. Add this onion mixture to the idli batter. Add baking soda and salt. Mix well.
  4. Grease the paniyaram pan with oil and pour the batter into each mold.
  5. Cook until they turn golden brown and crispy.
  6. Serve hot with coconut chutney or sambar.

Mysore pak

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup gram flour (besan)
  • 1 cup ghee
  • 1 cup sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

Instructions

  1. Heat ghee in a pan.
  2. Add gram flour and roast on low heat until aromatic.
  3. In a separate pan, combine sugar and water to make a syrup.
  4. Pour the syrup into the roasted gram flour and mix well.
  5. Pour the mixture into a greased tray and let it cool.
  6. Cut into pieces and serve.
